Virtual Reality (VR)

What is Virtual Reality? Virtual reality, or VR, is a three-dimensional, computer-generated environment that can be explored and interacted with by a person. That person becomes a part of that world, and can manipulate objects or perform a series of actions within the environment. Virtual reality is created using VR software and hardware, and it is typically experienced through a headset worn by the user.

The goal of VR is to immerse the user in a simulated environment that feels real and allows them to interact with it as if it were real. For example, in a VR game, the player may be able to pick up and throw objects, or move around the environment. In a VR training program, the user may be able to perform tasks within a realistic environment, such as fixing a machine or driving a car. VR has been used for decades, but it has only become widely available in the past few years. Oculus Rift and HTC Vive are two of the most popular VR headsets on the market, and there are many different VR programs and games available. Virtual reality has a number of potential applications, including education, training, entertainment, and more. For example, VR can be used to give users a realistic experience of what it would be like to drive a car, or to train surgeons in performing delicate operations. VR can also be used to create immersive, realistic games that provide an escape from reality.
